Vision

The traditional newspaper market is ripe for a change, and it has been since the 1990s. Ever since the advent of the internet people have been saying: “This is going to revolutionize the newspaper industry” but nothing much has happened since. Except for three things: Newspaper revenues are dropping to the floor, qualified staff are being laid off and the quality of reporting is just getting worse. We’ve followed these developments for some time now. We’ve seen enough of it.
are claiming people don’t pay for online content and editors are crucial because they know better what is relevant to readers. We think these people are full of it.
We believe in a world where people pay for high quality background pieces and solid journalism has a place.
To create this future we started Deepr. Join us on our journey to reinvent the future of quality journalism.
